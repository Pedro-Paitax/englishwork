The Evolution of Medicine

Precision Medicine

This quarter students were given the task of giving presentations on articles describing recent technological advances related to medicine and how they will affect not only the future of you, the reader, but also various people around the world. The article chosen for the team behind this paper was "Precision Medicine/Customized Medicine: critical analysis of the moves to transformation of biomedicine at the beginning of the 21st century", written by Jorge Alberto Bernstein Iriart and published in the journal "Cadernos de Saúde Pública", and it describes how precision medicine, or PM, works, its proposals, and its risks.

Precision medicine is, in short, a new form of medicine that promises to revolutionize traditional medicine by showing that instead of having a single drug for the population, it will analyze the genetic profile, or DNA, of the specific patient while deciding on a specific drug so that it will maximize the positive outcomes while doing its best to reduce the negative ones. While this proposal may seem incredibly attractive, there is one major problem that prevents it from going forward which, as always, is the lack of investment and the high cost of its drugs. That is, although its proposal is beneficial to the population, the lack of money is the reason behind its lack of popularity, one of the proofs of this being the use of this medicine in the group of more developed countries in which Brazil, unfortunately, is not part of.
