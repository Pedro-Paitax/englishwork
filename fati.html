Fati Love

The Path for The Happiness?

Who Said It?

The person who uses this thesis is the philosopher Nietzsche. Friedrich Nietzsche was a philologist (a scholar of classical languages ​​such as Latin and Ancient Greek), a contemporary German poet and philosopher, the author of a vast and controversial work. His books left the first signs of the emergence of contemporary philosophy. Nietzsche dedicated himself to the study of Judeo-Christian morality and operated a kind of comparison of societies before and after Christianity, having classified it as the central factor in the weakening of the human being in the modern era. Nietzsche can be considered an author whose life and biography influenced the production and reception of his work.

But what really is Fati Love?

A subject that generates a lot of controversy in philosophy classes, the theme "Fati love", from the Latin "love to destiny" takes us to an ecstasy of reflections where we see the need for a renewal in our soul. A shift to a new way of seeing things as they really are. I think acceptance is something very complex for many people, for myself a lot of the time, but it's something necessary. When we accept what life wants to bring us, we simply live. No worries. No regrets. In the most intense way possible. Just live. Only point that I partially agree with Nietzsche on his philosophy
